title: "The Broken Promise of Merit: NEET, Paper Leaks, and a System in Crisis"
---

We are taught from a very young age that if we keep our heads down, study hard, and clear the cut-offs, our futures will be secure. We sacrifice sleep, social lives, and our mental peace to prepare for competitive exams, believing in the fundamental promise of meritocracy. But what happens when the system itself is rigged?

The recent controversies surrounding the NEET exams have exposed the deep, systemic rot within our education evaluation boards. The National Testing Agency (NTA), an autonomous body entrusted with conducting these massive, life-altering exams seamlessly, has repeatedly shown it is incapable of securing a simple question paper. 

When paper leaks happen, it isn't just a logistical failure; it is a direct assault on the honest efforts of millions of aspirants. Imagine spending years grinding through mock tests and coaching modules, only to realize that someone else bought the exact paper a day before the exam for a few lakhs. 

The bureaucratic response only adds insult to injury. The initial, knee-jerk reaction from the Education Ministry and testing authorities is almost always denial. We are told the exams were conducted fairly, that rumors are just rumors. It takes widespread outrage, protests, and judicial intervention for the authorities to even admit there was a "compromise" in specific centers. 

This isn't just about NEET. This is a recurring nightmare across various state and national level examinations. It points to a terrifying lack of accountability. When the Education Minister addresses these issues, the common citizen doesn't want to hear bureaucratic jargon or defensive deflections. We want accountability. We want structural reform. We want to know that the people responsible for selling the futures of millions of youth are put behind bars.

Until the system respects the time, energy, and sheer desperation of the students sitting in those exam halls, the phrase "Education is the key to success" will remain nothing but a cruel joke for the common citizen.
